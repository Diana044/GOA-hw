Causes of World War I

The immediate cause of World War I was the assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand, heir to the Austro-Hungarian throne, on June 28, 1914, in Sarajevo. This event triggered a series of alliances and conflicts. However, underlying causes included:

  1. Militarism – Nations built up massive militaries, leading to an arms race.
  2. Alliances – Entangling alliances pulled many countries into conflict.
  3. Imperialism – Competition for colonies heightened tensions.
  4. Nationalism – Ethnic groups sought independence or dominance, particularly in the Balkans.

Key Countries and Their Motives

  1. Austria-Hungary
    • Why They Joined: Austria-Hungary declared war on Serbia after the assassination of Franz Ferdinand. They saw Serbia as a threat to their empire's stability due to Serbian nationalist movements
    • Goals: Suppress Serbian nationalism and maintain control over their diverse empire.
    • Allies: Germany, Ottoman Empire, Bulgaria (Central Powers).
  • Germany
    • Why They Joined: Germany supported Austria-Hungary due to their alliance and sought to strengthen their position in Europe.
    • Goals: Expand territorial control and counterbalance Russia, France, and Britain.
    • Allies: Austria-Hungary, Ottoman Empire, Bulgaria.
  • France
    • Why They Joined: France entered the war after Germany declared war on them. This was largely due to Germany's invasion of Belgium, violating neutralit
    • Goals: Regain Alsace-Lorraine, protect their borders, and weaken Germany
    • Allies: Britain, Russia, later joined by the United States and others (Allied Powers).
  • Russia
    • Why They Joined: Russia supported Serbia, a fellow Slavic nation, and wanted to counter Austria-Hungary's and Germany's growing influence.
    • Goals: Protect Slavic nations, expand influence in the Balkans, and secure warm-water ports.
    • Allies: France, Britain, Serbia, later Italy and others.
  • Britain
    • Why They Joined: Britain declared war on Germany after the violation of Belgium's neutrality, which Britain was bound to protect.
    • Goals: Prevent German dominance in Europe and maintain balance of power.
    • Allies: France, Russia, Serbia, later the U.S. and others.

    Franz Ferdinand

    • Background: Franz Ferdinand was the Archduke of Austria and heir to the Austro-Hungarian throne.
    • Role: His assassination in Sarajevo by Gavrilo Princip, a Bosnian Serb nationalist, was the immediate trigger for World War I.
    • Vision: Ferdinand supported reforms to give more autonomy to ethnic groups within the empire, which angered nationalist groups like the Black Hand in Serbia.
